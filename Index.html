About Brian Caper

Brian Caper is a skilled economic development professional with nearly 15 years of experience working in city economic growth, urban planning, and workforce development. He has a strong background in finance and urban and regional planning and holds an Executive MBA from the University of Florida.

Throughout his career, Brian Caper has worked on large redevelopment projects, public-private partnerships, and economic programs designed to support local communities. He has served in senior leadership positions, including Economic & Workforce Development Director and Managing Director for City Development, where he managed multimillion-dollar budgets, major infrastructure assets, and large redevelopment efforts.

Core Competencies of Brian Caper

1. Economic Development and Strategic Planning

Strong expertise in developing and implementing economic growth strategies that support long-term regional development. This includes identifying investment opportunities, supporting business attraction and retention, and designing policies that strengthen local economies. The focus is on using data-driven planning and financial insight to guide sustainable and balanced growth.

2. Urban Development and Infrastructure Management

Experienced in leading urban redevelopment and infrastructure initiatives that improve city spaces and support community needs. This involves coordinating with government bodies, private sector partners, and community stakeholders to deliver complex projects such as redevelopment areas, housing developments, and public infrastructure improvements. Strong capability in managing budgets, timelines, and multi-stakeholder projects ensures effective delivery.

3. Workforce Development and Community Economic Growth

Skilled in designing and supporting workforce development programs that connect people with employment opportunities and skill-building pathways. This includes aligning training programs with industry demand and promoting partnerships between educational institutions, employers, and public agencies. The goal is to strengthen workforce participation and ensure that economic growth benefits local communities.

My Mission

To drive inclusive and sustainable economic growth by leading strategic urban development, strengthening workforce systems, and fostering strong public-private partnerships that create long-term community value. The focus is on delivering practical, data-informed solutions that improve local economies while ensuring that development benefits residents, businesses, and future generations.

My Vision

To help build resilient, forward-looking cities where economic development, infrastructure, and workforce opportunities are fully integrated creating communities that are both economically competitive and socially equitable. The long-term aim is a model of urban growth where prosperity is shared, development is sustainable, and every resident has access to meaningful opportunity.
